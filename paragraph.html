<p> Explanation

The <p> element in HTML is used to define a paragraph. It is a block-level element, which means it starts on a new line and typically stretches across the full width of its containing element.

Here's a breakdown of how the <p> element works:

  • Opening Tag <p>: The <p> tag is the opening tag of the paragraph.
  • Content: The content of the paragraph goes between the opening <p> tag and the closing </p> tag. This content can include text, links, images, and other inline or block-level elements.
  • Closing Tag </p>: The </p> tag is the closing tag that marks the end of the paragraph.

The <p> element is commonly used to structure and organize textual content on a webpage. It helps browsers and other user agents understand that the enclosed content forms a distinct paragraph, providing visual separation and aiding in the readability of the document.
